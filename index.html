"roller derby"

The term “roller derby” dates to the 1920s, and was originally used to describe the roller skate races that transformed into Leo Seltzer’s touring competition. Seltzer's Transcontinental Roller Derby evolved from a marathon skating race on a banked track to a more physical competition.

Roller Games

As the sport gained momentum in the 50's and 60's, Leo Seltzer handed off his franchise to his son Jerry. Jerry introduced a theatric flair more akin to wrestling. The Roller Derby organization was shut down in 1973.

Modern Derby

In early 2000, a group of grassroots women revived the sport, giving modern flat track derby it's inception. Since 2004, the governing body of roller derby, as known as WFTDA, has introduced new rules to make the sport about athleticism and teamwork. Long gone are the days of theatrics and flair.

Recreational: Don't f$@k with our fun!

The Charleston Derby Syndicate (CDS) is a co-ed, noncompetitive recreational league created for the enjoyment, amusement and pleasure of all past and present roller derby veterans, inexperienced newcomers, and supporters in the greater Charleston area.

Co-ed: We're not NC!

The Charleston Derby Syndicate is comprised of men and women ages 18 and older who have a passion for roller derby but desire to skate or learn how to play roller derby without the pressure that comes from a competitive league.

All inclusive: No membership required.

Anyone, regardless of gender, race, creed, and league affiliation is welcome to attend Charleston Derby Syndicate practices and events.

Rules: We don't need no stinking rules!

Although the Charleston Derby Syndicate abides primarily, but not exclusively, by WFTDA rules and policies, the Charleston Derby Syndicate is currently not a member of any skating organization, and therefore was not created for interleague competition.
